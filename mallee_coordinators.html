How Mallee works

Built around
the I-CAN framework.

01

Participant nominates their support network

The participant shares the family members, friends, and informal carers who support them. Each person is enrolled directly — no coordinator admin required.

02

Mallee checks in, twice a week

A structured five-minute voice conversation with each support person. Contemporaneous, consistent, and captured in the participant's own words.

Mallee sounds like this…
"What did they manage to do well or independently today with the right support in place?"
"How are you feeling in yourself, not just as a carer, but as a person?"
03

Care Record builds automatically

Every conversation is structured, timestamped, and mapped to I-CAN functional domains. Support hours, carer observations, and notable events are captured across the full 13-week period.

04

Evidence is ready before the assessment

Your client arrives at their I-CAN assessment with months of structured, contemporaneous evidence — not a reconstruction. Transcripts available on request.

I-CAN assessments from mid-2026

Section 34(1)(e) requires
evidence of informal support.

The new I-CAN assessment framework requires the NDIA to consider what is reasonable to expect from a participant's family and informal networks. That means assessors will ask about care arrangements that are currently undocumented. Mallee gives your clients the contemporaneous, structured evidence to answer that question with confidence — not estimates reconstructed from memory on assessment day.

Registration Group 0124 pending. Aligned with Australia's National Carer Strategy 2024–2034.
