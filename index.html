For NDIS families preparing for I-CAN

Your I-CAN assessment is coming.
Walk in with evidence, not estimates.

From mid-2026, every NDIS participant will go through a new support needs assessment. Section 34(1)(e) of the NDIS Act requires the NDIA to consider what is reasonable to expect from families and family networks. This means the assessor will ask about your family care arrangements. Mallee removes the stress of a moment-in-time interview, ensuring you can explain your support needs with months of structured evidence behind you.

For you

You're already doing
two jobs.

Managing your condition. Coordinating your supports. And somewhere on top of all of that, collecting evidence, preparing for assessments, trying to document informal care that everyone can see but nobody's recording. That second job is exhausting. And it shouldn't be yours to carry.

Your support network, your mum, your partner, the neighbour who drives you on Tuesdays, is probably the most important part of your care. And it's almost certainly the least documented. Mallee checks in with your people, gently, twice a week, and builds the evidence record quietly in the background. So when your I-CAN assessment arrives, you walk in with months of proof. Not a memory test.

  • Your support network stays visible, your funding reflects reality
  • Months of documented evidence, not a single moment-in-time interview
  • No chasing paperwork, no reconstructing history, no drowning in the admin of your own condition
For your people

You didn't sign up
for the paperwork.

You show up because you love them. The morning routine, the appointments, the phone calls, the worry. You do it without being asked. And then, on top of all of it, you're expected to remember and document everything, in the language of a system you didn't choose and weren't trained to navigate.

That feeling of always being behind? It's not just you. It's the system.

Mallee takes the second job away. One gentle call, twice a week. While you're just talking, about your week, about how they're going, Mallee is quietly turning what you do into the documented record your family deserves. So you can spend your time being a mum, a partner, a mate. Not a record keeper.

  • One quick call, twice a week. That's the entire ask.
  • What you do gets seen. Every hour, every act of care, on record.
  • Your wellbeing is recorded, alongside the participant's.
Built from personal experience

The feeling of always
being behind.

My Dad has Corticobasal Degeneration. Progressive conditions don't get better. The trajectory is fixed. You stop spending your energy on paperwork and start spending it on the people you love.

What I kept hearing from families like mine was the same exhausting gap. There was always this feeling of being behind. Behind on the documentation, behind on the evidence, never quite able to prove what everyone could plainly see.

Mallee exists so that families like mine don't lose each other to that feeling. So the care is seen, counted, and sustained.
